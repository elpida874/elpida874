7 am

my mum picked me up in the early morning to get me to the train to ice - wonder - land.  first to the airport, of course. I was nervous, but the good type of it. like wtf I go to iceland. on my own. the first time. can't believe it until I'm there...-type of nervous. 

in front of us was a car...the sign...okay damn it, here HE is again. okay. it's okay for me. so he gets me to the airport. sweet. thank you. 

I tried to focus on me and my little adventure. not on the outside, weird signs about him and my recent past. so I was really excited for iceland and to see the northern lights. 

that was my only intention for this trip. 

until now. now, he was back. without me doing anything consciously to support this direction of my life, he was back. the calm feeling, really, really small underneath all other thoughts and feelings, this small feeling, this little expectation, maybe now, maybe in iceland...why not?

5 pm

hello reykjavik,

thank you for the best first afternoon, evening and night in a foreign city.

after a lovely drive with the bus from the airport to reykjavik, I checked in with my code and got into my room with another code.  the hostel I found was an old hospital, created into a hostel. thought, yeah this sounds fun. 

for my surprise I found myself in a 4 bed room, I booked a 6 bed room...lucky me.

there was one bunk bed at the left side, one single bed in the middle and one single one at the right side next to the window front.  I picked the right single bed. the bunk bed was occupied. the second one empty as well. 

had a wonderful view every morning. 

it was little after 5 pm, I started to unpack some stuff. 

the door opened loudly "velkomen in..." and my first contact during this journey (after a really nice, old, german lady on the bus from the airport) entered the stage.

a girl, looking like an escimo smiled at me: "Hi, I am fiona (name changed) from scotland. Just getting back from a tour..."

 I smiled back and after introducing ourselves to each other, she said she will go for a beer to a pub. I thought for a second, why not...and asked her if it's ok, when I join. 




so the first evening in reykjavik started. really good. 

I tried to live in every moment and to stay open to whatever will happen. 

what a good feeling. 

At this point of time I was actually through all books about the messages of the pleiadians...  and other stuff... 

so we got to the pub. I saw the streets of reykjavik for the first time.  

it was really good just to be yourself. nobody knows you here. 

on the way we got a hotdog (to be honest, I liked the taste, but they were not good for my stomach...). my credit card didn't work, the machine stopped by showing "please wait". so he was really nice the hotdog guy. he said okay he will wait and I said okay after one beer I will be back and pay in cash if it didn't work. 

yeah I saw him again the other day...we were a way too long at the pub... 

I stayed there for 8 days. and at the day of leaving, sitting in the plane, I got the message from my bankaccount, that this hotdog is paid...normally it's working synchron with my paid bills...

7 pm

the pub had a lovely entrance with coulored lights, a really nice smoking area - a leathercouch under a heater - and it was quite empty. perfect.

I don't like much people, so I mean crowded places. nothing for me. scares me a little bit.

so we entered, two girls in big ski pants, boots, warm scarfs and caps with ear lids.

after getting us two beers fiona decided that the sportchannel had to change to a soccer game of the celtics.

what should I say, she was so lovely and just her, the barkeeper changed the channel.

they had conversation about soccer and I had a seat in the middle of the pub.

it was really nice. good first evening for my first trip on my own.

plus the music was exactly mine. rock. old rock. new rock. classic rock. heavy rock. perfect mix.

of course fiona had an app to see where the next happy hours in reykjavik were over the evening. 

this pub was the first stop. without happy hour it's less fun to drink, cause of the prices...

I had my first icelandic beer, my first contact to new people. and I was still alive. plus I can do what I want. cause I am on my own.

this moment in the pub was an important one. for my growing. 

what should I say instead of just having one beer, we had a few more, some icelandic shots and my last one was a dark strong beer...  

met a girl, who is also a waitress. outside smoking and talking.  

after a few minutes talking to her, we had a fixpoint to start. she also was diagnosed with some mentall illness. 

we were hanging around at the pub until 12. fiona changed the bar with a really cute pair. the both were there their second night and the boy just proposed to her in iceland, that was the reason for their journey to the island. 

me and the girl were talking half of the night, about 1 am, the barkeeper closed and we said bye... 

this was kind of an soulfamily encounter for me.  

and what I didn't know at this time it wouldn't be the last during this trip.
